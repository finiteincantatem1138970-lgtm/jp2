KNOW THE CULTURE

More Than Just Food: A Philosophy of Life

Japanese cuisine, or *Japanese food*, is registered as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. It emphasizes seasonal ingredients, natural flavors, and presentation that reflects the beauty of nature. The core principles revolve around the "Five Colors" (white, black, red, yellow, green) and the "Five Flavors" (sweet, sour, salty, bitter, umami) to create a perfectly balanced meal.

Preparation often focuses on techniques like raw consumption (*sashimi*), boiling (*nimono*), grilling (*yakimono*), and deep-frying (*agemono*). Every dish is crafted to complement the others, offering a profound sensory experience.
